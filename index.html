04 — Access & Inequality

Who Gets to Swim?

Swimming is often portrayed as a universal sport — but access to lessons, facilities, and safe water is deeply unequal. CDC data reveals stark disparities in swimming ability and drowning risk by race.

Source: CDC — cdc.gov/drowning · Update with specific CDC figures on swimming ability by demographic

Takeaway: Structural barriers — cost of lessons, lack of nearby pools, historical exclusion — mean swimming has never been equally accessible. Climate change threatens to deepen these gaps.

06 — The Future

What Comes Next?

2030s

More frequent HABs close open-water venues. Pool maintenance costs rise as energy and water become more expensive in drought-prone regions.

2040s

Public pools in underfunded communities close at higher rates. Competitive swimming consolidates around well-resourced institutions.

2050

Swimming becomes a sport defined by access to resources — a luxury rather than a community activity. Who still gets to swim?

Questions to sit with:

  • Will pools become more expensive to maintain and access?
  • Who will still have access to safe swimming environments?
  • How can we design more equitable and sustainable aquatic infrastructure?
